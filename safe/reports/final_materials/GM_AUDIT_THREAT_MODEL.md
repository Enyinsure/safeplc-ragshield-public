# GM Audit Threat Model

## Attacker Capabilities

- Modify audit JSONL records after generation.
- Change query, action, answer, risk flags, evidence page, evidence hash, or M-EPI decision.
- Delete or replace local generated bundle files.
- Attempt to reuse a valid signature with modified payload content.

## Protection Goals

- Detect post-hoc modification of trusted multimodal query records.
- Bind payload, previous hash, signer identity, and public key fingerprint into the record hash.
- Allow independent verification without access to the original runtime process.

## Signature Coverage

The signed record hash covers timestamp, event type, signer_id, hash_algorithm, signature_algorithm, public_key_fingerprint, payload, and prev_hash for new records. Older records remain verifiable through backward-compatible hashing.

## What The Hash Chain Detects

The hash chain detects inserted, removed, reordered, or modified audit records when `prev_hash` or `record_hash` no longer matches.

## What The SM2 Signature Detects

The SM2 signature detects modification of the signed `record_hash`. A modified record cannot produce a valid signature unless the signer key is compromised.

## Independent Bundle Verification

Evidence bundles export the audit record body, record hash, public key, signature, and selected evidence hashes. A reviewer can verify the bundle offline with `python -m safe.trusted_rag.verify_evidence_bundle --latest`.

## What It Cannot Prevent

- Compromise of the local signing key.
- Malicious records generated by an already-compromised runtime.
- Physical deletion of all audit files without external backup.
- Replacement of the prototype cryptography with a malicious implementation.

## Production Upgrade Suggestions

本系统实现国密算法风格的可信审计原型，用于比赛原型验证；生产环境可替换为通过资质认证的国密密码模块或硬件密码机。

Recommended upgrades include key rotation, external timestamping, append-only storage, off-host log replication, and certified GM cryptographic modules.
